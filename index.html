Focus Trainer Privacy Policy

Effective date: June 23, 2026

Focus Trainer is a productivity timer app designed to help users manage focus sessions, break sessions, goals, achievements, XP and level progress, collectables, app usage rules, and difficulty settings.

Focus Trainer is designed as a local-first app. The app does not require a user account and does not operate a backend server for syncing timer, goal, XP, achievement, collectable, or app usage data.

If the user allows analytics, Focus Trainer may use Firebase Analytics to collect anonymous app usage events. Typed goal, habit, project, job, test, or description text is not collected through analytics.

1. Scope

This Privacy Policy applies to the Focus Trainer mobile application and its related app features, including timers, goals, achievements, collectables, analytics screens, notifications, app usage automation, screen blocking, and sharing features.

This Privacy Policy does not apply to third-party apps, websites, social media platforms, app stores, device manufacturers, operating system services, or services that users choose to interact with outside Focus Trainer.

2. Information Users Provide

Users may provide information inside the app, including:

  • Timer labels, session names, and block names
  • Goal names and goal duration settings
  • Difficulty, gender, mascot, theme, and notification preferences
  • Blocked app and allowed app lists

If a user contacts support by email, we may receive the email address, message content, attachments, and any other information the user chooses to include.

3. Information Stored Or Processed Locally

Focus Trainer may store or process the following information locally on the user's device:

  • Focus and break session elapsed time
  • Timer labels, timer history, and current active timer state
  • Session and block totals
  • Daily, weekly, and monthly analysis data
  • Goal progress, completion state, and claim state
  • Achievement progress, completion state, and claim state
  • XP, level progress, and collectable unlock state
  • User preferences and app difficulty mode
  • App usage timing used for automation, if Usage Access is enabled
  • Blocked and allowed app package rules, if configured by the user

This information is used to provide the app experience and is not sent to a Focus Trainer backend server.

4. Sensitive Permissions And Device Access

Usage Access

Usage Access may be used to detect app usage patterns for difficulty automation, app-based rules, and screen-blocking behavior.

When enabled, Focus Trainer may process app package names, usage timing, and app switching behavior locally on the device. This information is used to determine whether Focus Trainer should apply a rule, start or switch a timer, show an alarm, or enforce blocking behavior.

Focus Trainer does not use Usage Access to sell data, build advertising profiles, or send app usage data to a Focus Trainer server.

Overlay Or Screen Blocker Permission

Overlay or screen blocker permission may be used to display blocking screens, floating timer information, or strict-mode interruptions over other apps.

Notifications

Notifications may be used for active timer status, timer controls, timer completion alerts, reminders, alarms, or app behavior related to the selected difficulty mode.

Installed App Information

Focus Trainer may read or display installed app information locally so users can choose blocked or allowed apps for productivity rules.

5. How Focus Trainer Uses Information

Focus Trainer uses information to:

  • Run focus and break timers
  • Store timer progress and preserve active running timers
  • Reset and roll over daily timer state
  • Display session, block, day, week, and month analysis
  • Track goals, achievements, XP, and level progress
  • Unlock collectables based on level
  • Apply difficulty settings, app usage rules, alarms, and screen blocker behavior
  • Remember user preferences
  • Measure optional analytics events if the user allows analytics collection
  • Help diagnose support requests when users contact us

Optional Firebase Analytics

If the user selects "Allow analytics", Focus Trainer may send anonymous app usage events to Firebase Analytics, such as onboarding choices, the selected "where did you hear about us" option, whether the user said they plan to take a test, do a job, or work on a project, and successful creation of test, job, project, or habit items.

Focus Trainer does not send typed goal names, habit names, project names, job names, test names, descriptions, notes, or other open-ended text to Firebase Analytics.

6. Goals, Achievements, XP, And Collectables

Focus Trainer tracks goals, achievements, XP, levels, and collectables to provide progression features.

Goal and achievement data may include goal names, target durations, progress, completion status, reward claim status, and related timer activity.

XP from goals and achievements is added only when the user selects the completed item in the relevant app screen. Timer-based XP may be calculated from active focus activity according to the app's progression rules.

7. Native Sharing

Focus Trainer may allow users to share collectable images or related content.

When the user taps share, Focus Trainer hands the image or content to the device's native Android share sheet. The user then chooses the third-party app or service to receive the content.

Focus Trainer does not control how the selected third-party app processes shared content. Users should review the privacy policy of any third-party app they choose to share with.

8. Data Sharing And Disclosure

Focus Trainer does not sell personal information.

Focus Trainer does not operate a backend server that receives the user's timer, goal, XP, achievement, collectable, or usage-access data. If the user allows analytics, Firebase Analytics may receive the limited anonymous app usage events described above.

We may disclose information only in limited situations:

  • When the user chooses to share content through the native share sheet
  • When the user voluntarily contacts support
  • If required to comply with applicable law, legal process, or enforceable government request
  • To protect the rights, safety, or integrity of users, the app, or others

9. Third-Party Services

Focus Trainer may depend on standard platform services provided by Android, Google Play, device manufacturers, or the user's selected sharing apps.

These services may process information under their own privacy policies. For example:

  • Google Play may process app installation, crash, purchase, review, or device information according to Google's policies.
  • Firebase Analytics may process optional anonymous app usage events if the user allows analytics collection.
  • Android may handle permissions, notifications, usage access, overlay settings, and native sharing.
  • Third-party apps selected from the share sheet process shared content according to their own policies.

Focus Trainer is not responsible for the privacy practices of third-party services.

10. Data Retention

Focus Trainer keeps local app data for as long as needed to provide app functionality or until the user deletes it.

Local data may remain on the device until:

  • The user deletes app data through Android settings
  • The user uninstalls the app
  • The user resets or removes content inside the app
  • The app's daily rollover and cleanup logic resets or updates timer state
  • Future app features provide additional deletion or reset controls

11. Data Deletion And User Choices

Users can delete locally stored Focus Trainer data by uninstalling the app or clearing the app's storage through Android system settings.

Users can also manage permissions through Android settings, including:

  • Usage Access
  • Overlay or screen blocker permission
  • Notifications

Because Focus Trainer does not maintain a backend account system, deleting local app data on the device is generally the primary way to delete app data.

12. Security

Focus Trainer uses local device storage and Android platform permission controls to protect app data.

No method of storage or processing is perfect, and we cannot guarantee absolute security. Users should protect their devices with a passcode, biometric lock, or other device-level security features.

13. Children's Privacy

Focus Trainer is not intended for children under 13 years old, or the equivalent minimum age required by local law.

We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided information to us through support or another channel, contact focustrainersupport@gmail.com.

14. International Users And Regional Rights

Focus Trainer is designed to store productivity data locally on the user's device.

Depending on where a user lives, they may have rights to access, correct, delete, restrict, or object to certain processing of personal information.

Because Focus Trainer generally stores app data locally and does not maintain user accounts or a backend database, many privacy choices can be exercised directly on the device by deleting app data, uninstalling the app, or changing permissions.

15. Changes To This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time as Focus Trainer changes, new features are added, legal requirements change, or app store requirements change.

When we update this Privacy Policy, we will update the effective date at the top of the policy. If the changes are significant, we may also provide additional notice in the app or through another appropriate method.

16. Contact

For privacy questions, support requests, or deletion requests related to support emails, contact focustrainersupport@gmail.com.
